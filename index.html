FGGS-LiDAR: Ultra-Fast, GPU-Accelerated Simulation from General 3DGS Models to LiDAR

Abstract

FGGS-LiDAR bridges general 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) assets and high-performance LiDAR simulation. We introduce a prior-free 3DGS-to-mesh pipeline based on volumetric discretization and narrow-band TSDF, producing watertight, topologically consistent meshes from arbitrary pretrained 3DGS models—without LiDAR-specific supervision or architectural changes. Paired with a fully GPU-accelerated, BVH-based ray-casting engine, our simulator renders first-hit ranges at 500+ FPS on million-triangle scenes while preserving geometric fidelity.

On indoor/outdoor benchmarks, our simulations closely match scans rendered from real-scanned meshes (e.g., indoor CD ≈ 4.07 mm, F-score 0.994; outdoor CD ≈ 17.0 mm, F-score 0.982). The framework enables plug-and-play reuse of existing 3DGS assets for scalable, multi-modal simulation.
