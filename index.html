FGGS-LiDAR: Ultra-Fast, GPU-Accelerated Simulation from General 3DGS Models to LiDAR

Junzhe Wu1, Yufei Jia2, Yiyi Yan3, Zhixing Chen1, Tiao Tan1, Zifan Wang4, Guangyu Wang1, BoKui Chen1†, Guyue Zhou5†
1Tsinghua University (Shenzhen) · 2Tsinghua University (Beijing) · 3DISCOVER Robotics · 4HKUST (Guangzhou) · 5AIR, Tsinghua University
Paper (PDF) arXiv Code Video

Convert any pretrained 3DGS into a watertight mesh and run BVH-accelerated LiDAR ray-casting at real-time rates.

Abstract

FGGS-LiDAR bridges general 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) assets and high-performance LiDAR simulation. We introduce a prior-free 3DGS-to-Mesh pipeline based on volumetric discretization and narrow-band TSDF, producing watertight, topologically consistent meshes from arbitrary pretrained 3DGS models—without LiDAR-specific supervision or architectural changes. Paired with a fully GPU-accelerated, BVH-based ray-casting engine, our simulator renders first-hit ranges at 500+ FPS on million-triangle scenes while preserving geometric fidelity.

On indoor/outdoor benchmarks, our simulations closely match scans rendered from real-scanned meshes (e.g., indoor CD ≈ 4.07 mm, F-score 0.994; outdoor CD ≈ 17.0 mm, F-score 0.982). The framework enables plug-and-play reuse of existing 3DGS assets for scalable, multi-modal simulation.

Method

  1. 3DGS → Occupancy Volume (LBVH-accelerated): build AABB per Gaussian, construct LBVH, tile-wise cull & accumulate density, threshold to binary occupancy, extract surface voxels.
  2. Narrow-band TSDF & Isosurface: flood-fill outside labeling, layered distance propagation, truncation; Marching Cubes to a watertight mesh, then structure-first simplification and non-shrinking smoothing.
  3. GPU LiDAR Ray-Casting: per-beam BVH traversal with early termination; SoA layout, shared-memory caching, warp-coherent traversal for high throughput.
